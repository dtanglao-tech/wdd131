"Tusok-Tusok" Site Plan

A course project plan for an interactive guide to Filipino street food culture.

1. Site Name

Name: Tusok-Tusok: An Interactive Guide to Filipino Street Food

Reasoning: I chose "Tusok-Tusok" because it captures the exact feeling of eating street food in the Philippines. In Tagalog, it literally means "poke-poke"—which is exactly what you do with a sharp bamboo skewer when you're standing around a sizzling wok, picking out fresh fish balls or kwek-kwek. It’s a warm, casual, and universally recognized phrase that immediately sets an authentic and welcoming tone for the website.

2. Site Purpose

The goal of this website is to celebrate and share the vibrant world of Filipino street food culture. It acts as an engaging digital roadmap for both locals who love these snacks and curious foodies from around the world who want to know more about them. Visitors can dive into classic staples like taho, isaw, and banana cue, exploring their cultural backgrounds, flavor profiles, and traditional preparation methods. Behind the scenes, the site serves as a live portfolio piece demonstrating responsive CSS layouts and interactive, client-side JavaScript features—like dynamic flavor filtering and custom sauce pairing simulations—to create a smooth, user-friendly experience.

3. Target Audience Scenarios

  • Scenario 1: "I'm traveling to the Philippines for the first time and keep seeing street vendors. What exactly is a 'Kwek-Kwek', what does it taste like, and what kind of sauce am I supposed to dip it in?"
  • Scenario 2: "I have a major sweet tooth and I'm looking for a classic afternoon snack (merienda) that isn't heavy or savory. How can I quickly filter out the fried meat options to find sweet treats like Taho or Banana Cue?"

4. Color Schema

The palette mimics the warm, inviting, golden-fried tones of street food carts, balanced by crisp accents for clear modern UI readability.

Primary: #E76F51 (Sweet Chili Orange) - Headings & Accents
Secondary: #F4A261 (Golden Cue Amber) - Cards & Highlights
Background: #FAFAFA (Soft Canvas White) - Clean Page canvas
Text: #264653 (Deep Charcoal) - Readable Text & Footers

5. Typography

Heading Font: Fredoka

Used for primary titles and main headings (`h1`, `h2`, `h3`). Its rounded, friendly look perfectly matches the fun, casual, and communal energy of street food culture.

Body Font: Quicksand

Used for all paragraphs, navigation links, and descriptive UI elements. It offers crisp legibility on small mobile displays while maintaining a modern geometric look.
